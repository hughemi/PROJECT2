COVID-19 Vaccination Rates in Australia and How We Can Help Other Regions

This page presents the success Australia are having with double vaccinating the population, then highlights the vaccine surplus Australia will have by 2023, and finally identifies the regions who will benefit most from our surplus of vaccines.

All data and references to 'vaccination rates' thoughout this page refer to double vaccination rates of a population against COVID-19.

You are also encouraged to interact with all of the graphs, especially the legends.



Australia, and the success they are having in reaching 80% double vaccination rates by the end of 2021

Unlike many countries in Sub-Saharan Africa whose outlook looks concerning and needs additional help, the outlook is quite the opposite in Australia.

Where 80% of a state's 16+ y.o population is double vaccinated, freedoms such as international travel and loosening of restrictions will be implemented. All states are on track to reach this figure by the end of 2021.

The predictions in the dashed lines below are simple trend generated forecasts that will not be extremely accurate. However, their purpose is serve as a rough guideline of when each state can expect to reach the 80% milestone.

How long until each state reaches 80% vaccinated

Currently Australia's double vaccination rates is roughly 62%.

The dashed lines are basic trend forecasts that serve as a guideline, and should not be taken seriously.

However, Australia and its states should all reach 80% vaccination rates by December 2021.

A timeline of Australia's vaccine supply

Australia are contracted to receive 250 million doses by mid 2023, however, only 75M are required to triple vaccinate the population. And 100M will be supplied to SE Asia & Pacific.

This leaves Australia with an excess of 75M doses. Should Australia donate these doses to regions of need, maybe Africa?

Which continents and countries are struggling to get their population vaccinated?

As a continent, Africa has a 4% double vaccination rate. Could the African region hold the world back from eliminating COVID and opening all borders?

It is a question that should be asked to all high-income, high-vaccinated countries.
