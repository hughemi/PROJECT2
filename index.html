COVID-19 Vaccination Rates in Australia and Other Regions

All data and references to 'vaccination rates' thoughout this webpage refer to the percentage of the eligible population that are double vaccinated against COVID-19.

You are also encouraged to interact with all of the graphs, especially the legends to better understand the topic.

Australia's recent success in getting the population double vaccinated

Australia is currently sitting at 62.4% vaccinated for the population of 16+ years old. With all states above 50%.

Where 80% of a state's 16+ y.o population is double vaccinated, freedoms such as international travel and loosening of restrictions will be implemented.

The predictions in the graph below are simple trend generated forecasts that are not extremely accurate. However, their purpose is to serve as a guideline of when each state can expect to reach the 80% milestone.

When will each state be 80% vaccinated?

Australia and its states should all reach the 80% vaccinated milestone by December 2021.

It seems the states who have experienced more COVID cases (NSW, ACT, VIC) will reach the 80% first.

Although more research would have to confirm.

A timeline of Australia's vaccine supply

Australia are contracted to receive 250 million doses by mid 2023, however, only 75M are required to triple vaccinate the population. And 100M will be supplied to SE Asia & Pacific.

This leaves Australia with an excess of 75M doses. Should Australia donate these doses to regions of need, maybe Africa?

Which regions are struggling to get their population vaccinated?

As a continent, Africa has a 4% double vaccination rate. Could the African region hold the world back from eliminating COVID and opening all borders?

It is a question that should be asked to all high-income, high-vaccinated countries. Australia is already sending millions of vaccines to the Pacific and Southeast Asia which, but with our extreme surplus of vaccines evidenced in previous graphs, should Australia look at assisting African countries as well?

Are vaccination rates at least increasing in Africa?

Compared to the rest of the world, Africa's vaccinations are low and slowly increasing.
